### 3. Ideal Customer Profiles — Primary & Secondary
**What it is:** Two fully developed Ideal Customer Profiles, each with:
- **Demographics** — Role, company size, industry, geography, budget range
- **Psychographics** — Values, aspirations, fears, self-image, worldview
- **Pain Points** — The specific problems that make them search for a solution like yours
- **Goals** — What success looks like for them (not what you think it looks like)
- **Buying Journey** — How they typically discover, evaluate, and select a provider
- **Language Patterns** — The words and phrases your audience likely uses, based on industry and psychographic patterns (not your internal jargon)
- **Objections** — What makes them hesitate, and how to address it
- **Where to Find Them** — Recommended channels, communities, and content formats based on audience patterns in your industry

**Why it matters:** Generic audience targeting is the most expensive mistake in marketing. "Small business owners" isn't a target audience — it's a census category. Two well-developed ICPs let you market with surgical precision: specific messages to specific people through specific channels.

**How it helps your business:** Your Primary ICP is who you should pursue most aggressively — the customer who's the best fit, highest value, and most likely to buy. Your Secondary ICP is your expansion opportunity — a different segment that also benefits from your offering but requires different messaging. Together, they cover 80%+ of your revenue potential while eliminating waste from broad targeting.

**What you'll see:** Two complete ICP documents, each 1–2 pages, ready to share with your team or agency.

### 18. Conversion Strategy & CTA Hierarchy
**What it is:** An analysis of how trust is built in your customer relationship and a three-tier CTA hierarchy:
- **Primary CTA** — Your main conversion action (book a call, start a trial, buy now)
- **Secondary CTA** — For interested-but-not-ready prospects (download a guide, join a webinar)
- **Tertiary CTA** — For cold or cautious visitors (subscribe to newsletter, follow on social)

**Why it matters:** Most businesses have one CTA: "Contact Us." That works for the 3% of visitors ready to buy right now — and loses the other 97%. A CTA hierarchy captures leads at every level of intent, building a pipeline that nurtures people toward the primary action over time.

**How it helps your business:** More conversion paths = more conversions. When a visitor isn't ready to buy, give them a low-commitment next step instead of losing them entirely. Over time, the secondary and tertiary CTAs feed your primary conversion pipeline.

**What you'll see:** A three-tier CTA framework with specific copy recommendations for each tier.
